What to Expect When Buying a Parrot in Spain

Purchasing an exotic parrot in Spain requires some preparation. Here is what you should know before making your decision:

Legal Requirements

Most parrot species sold in Spain are protected under CITES (Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species). This means you need to ensure that any parrot you purchase comes with a valid CITES certificate or a legal breeding declaration (for captive-bred birds that are exempt). At ParaisoDeAves, we handle all documentation transparently so you can adopt with confidence.

Health Certification

All our birds are checked by qualified avian veterinarians before transfer. We recommend that new owners also schedule a vet visit within the first week of bringing the bird home to establish a health baseline.

Age and Socialization

We typically offer birds between 7 and 15 months old — old enough to be independent eaters and young enough to form strong bonds with new owners. Every bird has been handled daily since hatching, making the transition to a new home much smoother.

Parrot Care: What You Need to Know

Owning a parrot in Spain is a long-term commitment. Depending on the species, parrots can live 20 to 80 years. Before buying, make sure you can provide:

  • A spacious cage with room to spread wings and climb
  • Daily social interaction — parrots are flock animals and need company
  • A varied diet of fresh vegetables, fruits, quality pellets and limited seeds
  • Mental stimulation through toys, puzzles and training
  • Regular veterinary check-ups with an avian specialist
  • A stable routine — parrots thrive on predictability
