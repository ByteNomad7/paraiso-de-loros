❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you want to know about buying an exotic parrot from ParaisoDeAves — availability, documentation, delivery, pricing and care.

About Our Birds

What parrot species do you have available?

We specialize in a wide range of exotic parrots including:

  • African Grey Parrot (Congo and Timneh)
  • Macaws — Blue & Gold, Scarlet, Hyacinth, Green-Winged, Military
  • Cockatoos — Umbrella, Sulphur-Crested, Moluccan, Galah
  • Amazon Parrots, Eclectus, Conures
  • Fertile eggs (by season)

Availability changes frequently. Contact us for the current stock list.

Are your birds hand-raised and socialized?

Yes — all our birds are hand-raised from hatching with daily human contact. This means they arrive in their new homes trusting, calm and ready to bond. We do not sell birds that have not been properly socialized. A well-socialized bird is the foundation of a good relationship with its new owner.

Are your birds captive-bred or wild-caught?

All our birds are captive-bred. We do not participate in the wild-caught bird trade under any circumstances. Every bird comes with breeding records and documentation proving its captive-bred origin.

What ages are the birds you sell?

We typically sell birds between 7 and 15 months of age. This age range means the birds are fully weaned and independent eaters, but still young enough to form strong bonds with new owners. We occasionally have older birds available — contact us for details.

Can I see photos and videos of my specific bird before buying?

Absolutely. We send real photos and videos of the actual bird you would be purchasing — not stock images. Transparency is one of our core commitments. You will know exactly who you are getting before you make any payment.

Pricing and Payment

How much do your parrots cost?

  • Conures: €400 – €800

  • African Grey Parrot: €1,200 – €2,500
  • Macaws (Blue & Gold, Scarlet): €1,500 – €3,500

  • Cockatoos: €800 – €3,000+

Contact us for exact current pricing on specific birds. All prices include CITES documentation and veterinary health certificate.

What does the price include?

Our price includes the bird, all CITES documentation, the EU veterinary health certificate, microchip or closed-ring identification, and our care starter guide. Transport costs are quoted separately based on your location and chosen delivery method.

Do I need to pay a deposit?

Yes. To reserve a specific bird, we require a deposit (typically 30–40% of the total price). This holds the bird for you while we finalize documentation and arrange delivery. Deposits are non-refundable if the buyer cancels, but fully transferable to another bird.

Legal Documentation & CITES

Are your birds CITES certified?

Yes. We provide full CITES compliance documentation with every bird. For Appendix I species (e.g., Hyacinth Macaw, some cockatoos), this is an EU Article 10 Annex A certificate. For captive-bred Appendix II species (African Grey, most macaws, Amazon parrots), this is a CITES certificate or legal breeding declaration. We explain all documentation in detail before purchase.

What documentation comes with my bird?
  • EU Veterinary Health Certificate
  • CITES export documentation (species-appropriate)
  • Microchip or closed-ring certificate
  • Breeding records and provenance documentation
  • ParaisoDeAves care guide
Do I need any permits to import a parrot to my country?

Within the EU, no import permit is needed for captive-bred birds with proper documentation — free movement applies. For non-EU countries (e.g., Switzerland, UK, Norway), an import permit from your country's authority is typically required. We advise buyers on this and assist with the application process where possible.

Delivery & Shipping

Do you ship parrots to other European countries?

Yes. We ship regularly to France, Germany, Portugal, Italy, Netherlands, Belgium, Austria, Switzerland, Sweden, Denmark, Ireland, Poland and more. See our buy parrots in Europe page for full details. See our delivery & shipping page for transport methods and process.

How are the birds transported?

We use IATA-approved live animal crates and specialist avian couriers. Within Spain, ground transport in temperature-controlled vehicles is available. International shipments go by air freight using airlines with established live animal programs. We also offer accompanied transport for premium birds.

Can I collect my bird in person?

Yes — and we encourage it for buyers within driving distance. You get to see where your bird was raised, meet the bird in its environment, and begin building rapport before the journey home. Collection is by appointment.

After Your Purchase

Do you offer after-sale support?

Yes. Our relationship with buyers does not end at the sale. We are available via WhatsApp and email for questions about diet, behaviour, health, training and veterinary referrals — indefinitely. We genuinely care about the wellbeing of every bird we place and want to support you for the bird's lifetime.

What should I prepare before my parrot arrives?

Before your bird arrives, you should have:

  • An appropriately sized cage with multiple perches
  • Species-appropriate food (pellets, fresh vegetables)
  • A few toys for initial enrichment
  • A quiet room for the first few days
  • Contact details for a local avian veterinarian

Read our full parrot care guide for detailed preparation advice.

How do I find an avian vet?

We recommend looking for a veterinarian who specializes in avian medicine or exotic animals. In Spain, ask for a "veterinario especialista en aves exóticas." We can recommend avian vets in major Spanish cities — just ask. Schedule a baseline health check within the first week of arrival, even if your bird seems perfectly healthy.
