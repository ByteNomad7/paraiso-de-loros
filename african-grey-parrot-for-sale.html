Why the African Grey Is the World's Most Sought-After Parrot

Among all parrot species available for sale in Europe, the African Grey parrot consistently tops the list of the most desired — and for good reason. Scientific studies have shown that African Greys demonstrate cognitive abilities comparable to a human child of 4 to 6 years old. They can learn hundreds of words, use language contextually, solve puzzles, and form emotional connections that last decades.

Whether you are an experienced parrot owner or someone making their first serious investment in an exotic bird, the African Grey offers unmatched companionship. They watch, learn, and respond in ways that will constantly surprise and delight you.

Congo vs. Timneh: Which African Grey Is Right for You?

Congo African Grey (CAG)

The larger of the two subspecies, with striking bright red tail feathers and silver-grey plumage. The Congo is slightly more outgoing and often begins talking earlier than the Timneh. They are the most commonly available African Grey for sale in Spain and Europe.

  • Size: 33–41 cm, 400–650g
  • Tail: bright red
  • Personality: social, slightly bolder
  • Talking ability: exceptional

Timneh African Grey (TAG)

Smaller and darker with a maroon-coloured tail and horn-coloured upper mandible. Timnehs are known for being slightly calmer and often begin socializing with strangers more readily. They may start talking even earlier than the Congo in some cases.

  • Size: 28–33 cm, 275–375g
  • Tail: maroon-dark grey
  • Personality: calm, adaptable
  • Talking ability: exceptional, often earlier

Caring for an African Grey Parrot

Social Needs

African Greys are highly social birds that need daily interaction. Without sufficient mental stimulation and companionship, they can develop stress behaviours like feather plucking. Plan to spend at least 2–3 hours per day in active or passive company with your Grey.

Diet

A well-balanced diet is critical. The base should be high-quality pellets (60–70%), supplemented with fresh vegetables (leafy greens, carrots, sweet potato), a moderate amount of fruit, and limited seeds as treats. African Greys are prone to calcium and vitamin A deficiency — variety is key.

Mental Stimulation

Rotate toys weekly, provide foraging puzzles, and engage in training sessions using positive reinforcement. African Greys can learn to perform tasks, identify objects, and even do basic arithmetic — keeping their minds active is essential for their wellbeing.

Veterinary Care

Find an avian vet before bringing your Grey home. Annual check-ups, beak and nail trims, and occasional blood panels keep your bird healthy for decades. We can recommend avian vets in major Spanish cities.

CITES Documentation Explained

African Grey parrots are listed on CITES Appendix I, meaning their international trade is strictly regulated. However, captive-bred birds within the EU can be legally sold with an EU Annex A certificate issued by the competent authority. At ParaisoDeAves, every African Grey we offer comes with:

  • EU Article 10 Annex A certificate (or equivalent breeder declaration)
  • Veterinary health certificate
  • Ring or microchip identification
  • Complete provenance and breeding records

For buyers outside Spain, we also prepare the necessary export documentation and coordinate with your country's import authority where required.

Is an African Grey the Right Bird for You?

An African Grey is a wonderful bird — but not for everyone. Before you buy, honestly consider:

  • Do you have 2–3 hours daily for interaction? Greys do not thrive alone.
  • Can you commit for 40–60 years? These birds are lifelong companions.
  • Do you have patience for a sensitive bird? Greys can be moody and need consistent, calm handling.
  • Can you afford ongoing costs? A quality diet, vet care and enrichment are non-negotiable.

If you answered yes to the above, the African Grey will reward you with one of the most extraordinary relationships possible between a human and a bird. Read our guide on best parrots for beginners if you are still weighing your options.
