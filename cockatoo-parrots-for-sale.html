Why Choose a Cockatoo?

Cockatoos are often described as the "love birds" of the parrot world. Unlike many other parrot species that maintain a certain independence, cockatoos actively seek physical contact, affection and companionship. They will often demand to be held, pet and included in family activities — which makes them extraordinarily rewarding for owners who can provide the time and attention they need.

Their dramatic crests, which rise and fall to express emotion, make cockatoos uniquely expressive birds. A raised crest can signal excitement, surprise or alarm; a flattened crest often means contentment. Learning to read your cockatoo's body language is one of the joys of owning one.

In Europe, cockatoos for sale are in high demand precisely because of these personality traits. At ParaisoDeAves, we specialize in hand-raising multiple cockatoo species to ensure they are confident, trusting and ready to bond from the moment they arrive in your home.

Cockatoo Ownership: The Honest Guide

Cockatoos are genuinely rewarding birds, but they are not for everyone. Here is an honest look at the pros and challenges:

✅ Benefits

  • Deeply affectionate and bonded companions
  • Highly expressive and entertaining
  • Can learn tricks, speech and routines
  • Beautiful, dramatic appearance
  • Very long lifespan — decades of companionship

⚠️ Challenges

  • Very loud — screaming is natural
  • Can develop feather-destructive behaviours if neglected
  • Produce significant feather dust (allergies possible)
  • Need 3–4+ hours of daily interaction
  • Expensive to care for properly long-term

Cockatoo vs Macaw — Key Differences

Two of the most popular large parrots for sale in Europe, cockatoos and macaws appeal to different owner profiles. The key differences:

  • Affection: Cockatoos are generally more physically affectionate and needy for contact; macaws tend to be more independent
  • Feather dust: Cockatoos produce enormous amounts of powder down — an issue for people with allergies or asthma. Macaws produce much less.
  • Talking: Both can talk, but macaws are generally considered stronger mimics
  • Size/space: Many cockatoo species need slightly less space than large macaws
  • Destructiveness: Both species chew heavily — provide appropriate wooden toys for both

Cockatoo Health: What You Need to Know

Cockatoos are susceptible to several health conditions. As a responsible owner you should be aware of:

  • Feather Destructive Behaviour (FDB): Often caused by boredom, stress or insufficient social contact. Providing enrichment and companionship prevents this.
  • Psittacine Beak and Feather Disease (PBFD): A viral disease affecting feathers and beak. Always request viral screening results from your breeder.
  • Aspergillosis: A fungal respiratory infection. Ensure clean air, good ventilation and no damp conditions.
  • Obesity: Cockatoos love seed but a seed-only diet leads to nutritional deficiencies and obesity. Feed pellets and fresh food as the base diet.
