The Delivery Process to Bristol

1. Reserve your bird

Contact us at paraisodeloros@gmail.com to discuss available birds, pricing, and timing. A deposit confirms your reservation. We send photos and video updates of your bird throughout its preparation period.

2. Documentation preparation

We prepare all Spanish CITES export documentation, the veterinary health certificate, and coordinate any import documentation required for the United Kingdom. For CITES Appendix I species, you will need a UK/IE import permit — we guide you through this process step by step.

3. Air cargo to Bristol Airport (BRS)

Your bird travels in an IATA-compliant live animal crate via specialist live-animal air freight. The journey from our aviary to Bristol Airport (BRS) typically takes 12–18 hours total. We monitor the process throughout and keep you updated.

4. Border clearance and collection

On arrival at Bristol Airport (BRS), your bird goes through border inspection. Once cleared — typically 2–4 hours after arrival — you collect from the cargo terminal with your identification and import documentation. We provide exact instructions in advance.

Note: Not all airports have Border Control Posts equipped for live animal inspection. We will confirm the appropriate collection point for your specific bird and location when we process your order. Bristol is a route we have experience with.

Frequently Asked Questions — Bristol Delivery

How long does delivery to Bristol take?

The air cargo journey from our aviary to Bristol Airport (BRS) typically takes 12–18 hours from point of departure. The total process from when you place your deposit to when your bird arrives is typically 4–12 weeks, depending on documentation processing times and flight scheduling.

Can I track my bird during transit?

Yes. We provide you with the air waybill number once the bird is loaded. You can track the cargo status online, and we maintain contact with the freight forwarder throughout and relay updates to you directly.

What if something goes wrong during transit?

We use specialist live-animal freight handlers with extensive experience in psittacine transport. Each crate is prepared for the birds' welfare throughout the journey. In the extremely rare event of a problem, we have protocols in place and insurance cover for the transit period.

Do I need to arrange anything on the Bristol side?

For Appendix II species, the main requirement on your side is your presence at the collection point with valid ID. For Appendix I species, you will need a UK/IE CITES import permit — we guide you through this process well in advance of the bird's travel date.

Can I visit the aviary in Spain before purchasing?

Yes — visits to our facility in Llíria, Valencia are warmly welcomed. Many UK and Irish buyers combine a visit with a Spain trip.
