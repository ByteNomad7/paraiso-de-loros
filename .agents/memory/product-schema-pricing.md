---
name: Product structured data pricing gaps
description: Which pages carry schema.org Product markup and the price/offers fields Google Search Console flags as invalid/incomplete.
---

Product JSON-LD exists on: `available-birds/*.html` (9, already complete with AggregateOffer lowPrice/highPrice), `fr/*/index.html` species pages (8), `pt/*/index.html` species pages (10), `tienda.html` (4 Products in an ItemList), and `criadero-de-loros-espana.html` (6 Products nested in an Organization `hasOfferCatalog`).

**Why it broke:** FR/PT species pages had bare Product objects (name/description/brand only, no `offers` at all) — likely because they were generated by a template pass that predates the pricing/offers convention used on the ES `available-birds/` pages. `tienda.html`'s offers had `priceCurrency`/`availability` but no `price`. Google's Product snippets report requires either `price` or `priceSpecification.price` in every offer, and flags missing `aggregateRating`/`review` as an appearance improvement (not an error).

**How to apply:** When adding a new Product schema, always include `offers` with `priceCurrency` + `price` (or `lowPrice`/`highPrice`/`offerCount` for AggregateOffer) — mirror the pattern in `available-birds/*.html`. Do not add fake `aggregateRating`/`review` data to silence the "improve appearance" suggestions — that violates Google's structured-data policy on fabricated reviews; only add these once real customer reviews exist.
