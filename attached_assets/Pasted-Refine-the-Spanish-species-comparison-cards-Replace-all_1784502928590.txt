Refine the Spanish species comparison cards.

Replace all generic emoji or symbolic icons such as hearts, puzzle pieces, brains, controllers and similar decorative icons with a small real image of the matching bird species.

Examples:

- Cacatúa Blanca card → use a small thumbnail of a White/Umbrella Cockatoo
- Cacatúa de Goffin card → use a small thumbnail of a Goffin’s Cockatoo
- Yaco card → use a small thumbnail of an African Grey
- Guacamayo Azul y Amarillo card → use a matching Blue-and-Gold Macaw thumbnail
- Amazona card → use the correct Amazon parrot species image

DESIGN

- Place the bird thumbnail to the left of the species name.
- Use a circular or softly rounded image.
- Recommended size: 48–56 px desktop and 42–48 px mobile.
- Use object-fit: cover.
- Keep the bird’s head and upper body clearly visible.
- Add a subtle border and soft shadow.
- Do not stretch, distort or crop the bird awkwardly.
- Keep all card headers aligned consistently.
- Maintain enough space between the image, title and scientific name.

IMAGE ACCURACY

- Every image must match the exact species named on the card.
- Do not use a generic parrot image for multiple species.
- Reuse existing approved website images when available.
- Do not invent or use unrelated species.
- If no verified species image is available, use the existing Paraíso de Aves logo mark temporarily rather than an emoji.

ACCESSIBILITY

- Add descriptive Spanish alt text, for example:
  - “Cacatúa blanca”
  - “Cacatúa de Goffin”
  - “Yaco africano”
- Decorative image wrappers must not interfere with keyboard navigation.

PERFORMANCE

- Use optimized WebP or AVIF thumbnails.
- Define explicit width and height.
- Lazy-load below-the-fold images.
- Avoid layout shift.

Keep the small descriptive trait pills below the text, but remove emoji characters from those pills as well. Replace them with clean text-only labels or subtle professional SVG icons.

The final result must feel like a premium breeder website, not a generic template.
