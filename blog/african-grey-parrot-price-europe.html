African Grey Parrot Price in Europe (2026) — Honest Guide

How much should you pay for an African Grey parrot in Europe? This honest, up-to-date guide covers typical prices across Spain, France, Germany and the rest of the EU — and what price extremes mean.

The African Grey is consistently one of the most sought-after — and most valuable — parrot species in Europe. If you are researching prices before making a purchase, this guide will give you a realistic picture of what to expect to pay, what drives price variation, and how to tell whether a deal is suspicious.

What Drives African Grey Parrot Prices?

1. Subspecies

Congo African Greys (Psittacus erithacus erithacus) are slightly larger and more common in Europe than Timneh Greys (Psittacus erithacus timneh), making them typically the slightly pricier option. Both are exceptional birds — the choice between them is largely personal preference. See our detailed African Grey parrot page for a side-by-side comparison.

2. Age and Socialization Level

Young birds (7–15 months) that have been hand-raised and extensively socialized command the highest prices — and for good reason. The investment in hand-raising a bird properly is significant. An adult bird being rehomed may cost less, but its history, temperament and adaptability to a new home can be unpredictable.

3. Documentation Quality

Full CITES documentation, an EU health certificate, microchip or ring identification and complete breeding records cost the breeder money to obtain and maintain. A bird sold without proper documentation should be cheaper — but this is a false economy. Legal risks to the buyer of purchasing an undocumented bird can far outweigh any saving.

4. Breeder Reputation

Established breeders with verified track records, professional aviaries and ongoing customer support can command a premium. This premium is generally justified — the risk of health problems, behavioural issues and documentation problems is significantly lower when buying from a reputable source.

5. Country of Purchase

Prices in Western Europe (Germany, France, Netherlands, Switzerland) tend to be higher than in Spain due to higher operational costs, regulatory overhead and market dynamics. Buying from a Spanish breeder and importing to your EU country can be a cost-effective option if done properly — see our buy parrots in Europe page for details.

When Should You Be Suspicious?

Prices below €600 for an African Grey are a major red flag. At this price point it is almost impossible for a breeder to have covered the legitimate costs of hand-raising, veterinary care, and proper documentation. These birds are very likely to be: (a) wild-caught and illegally sourced, (b) sick or malnourished, or (c) sold without documentation that could result in confiscation.

Other warning signs of suspicious pricing:

  • Price is "negotiable by 50%" — legitimate breeders have real costs and limited margin
  • Seller claims CITES documentation exists but cannot produce it before sale
  • Bird has no ring or microchip identification
  • Seller is vague about where the bird was bred and how
  • Communication is only by messaging app with no verifiable identity or physical address
  • Seller has multiple different species always available with no wait list

The True Cost of Owning an African Grey

Before fixating on purchase price, remember to factor in the ongoing cost of ownership. An African Grey can live 40–60 years. The purchase price is a one-time cost; feeding, housing, enrichment and veterinary care are annual commitments. Approximate annual costs in Spain:

  • Pellets and fresh food: €200–€400/year
  • Veterinary care (annual check-up): €80–€200
  • Toys and enrichment: €100–€250/year
  • Cage maintenance, cleaning: €50–€100/year

Over 40 years, the ongoing costs vastly exceed the purchase price. Buy the best bird you can afford from the most reputable breeder you can find — the upfront investment is the smallest part of what you will spend.
