Where to Buy Exotic Birds in Europe — A Trustworthy Guide

Finding a safe, legal and ethical source for exotic parrots in Europe requires research and vigilance. This guide explains exactly where to look — and where to avoid.

The European market for exotic birds — particularly parrots — is large and diverse. There are excellent breeders, established aviculturists and specialist importers operating across Spain, France, Germany, the Netherlands and beyond. But there are also bad actors who deal in undocumented, wild-caught or poorly cared-for birds. Knowing where to look and what to look for is essential to buying a healthy, legal bird that will thrive in your home.

Where to Find Exotic Birds in Europe

✅ Specialist Breeders (Best Option)

Dedicated parrot breeders with purpose-built aviaries are the gold standard for buying exotic birds in Europe. They know their birds individually, can provide full provenance documentation, offer genuine after-sale support, and have a direct financial and reputational interest in placing birds appropriately.

Spain is one of Europe's strongest parrot breeding countries. Portuguese, Italian and Dutch breeders are also well-regarded. When evaluating a specialist breeder, look for: a professional website, real social media presence, verifiable reviews and willingness to answer detailed questions about breeding conditions and socialization.

✅ Aviculture Societies and Bird Clubs

National and regional bird societies (such as Spain's bird keeping associations) can be excellent places to find reputable breeders through member networks. Member breeders in established societies are generally subject to a code of practice and peer accountability. Ask for a list of member breeders in your country and region.

✅ Specialist Avian Retailers

A small number of high-quality, specialist avian retailers in Europe maintain their own breeding programs or work exclusively with verified breeders. These are not the same as general pet shops — they focus entirely on birds and maintain professional standards. Ask about the source of their birds and request full documentation before purchase.

⚠️ General Online Classified Ads (High Risk)

Platforms like Wallapop, Milanuncios, Leboncoin, eBay Kleinanzeigen and similar classifieds carry high risk for exotic bird buyers. While legitimate sales do occur through these platforms, the level of due diligence required is significant and the risk of encountering scammers, undocumented birds or mistreated animals is elevated. If you use these platforms, never proceed without physically verifying the bird, its documentation and the seller's identity.

🚫 Social Media Groups Without Verification

Facebook groups and WhatsApp communities dedicated to parrot sales are unregulated and frequently used by sellers of wild-caught or undocumented birds. They can also be vectors for online scams where photos of parrots are used to steal deposits. Exercise extreme caution and never send money without having physically seen the bird and verified all documentation.

🚫 Exotic Bird Markets (Without Documentation)

Some countries still have informal bird markets or fairs where exotic species are sold without adequate documentation. While some legitimate sellers operate at these events, the environment makes verification difficult and the risk of purchasing wild-caught or illegally sourced birds is significant. The EU is progressively tightening enforcement in this area.

Buying Exotic Birds Across EU Borders

One of the significant advantages for buyers in the EU is the ability to purchase birds across borders within the single market. A buyer in France can legally buy from a breeder in Spain, and movement within the EU is possible with proper documentation. Here's how it works:

Within the EU (Free Movement)

For captive-bred exotic birds moving between EU member states, no import permit is required. The bird needs:

  • An EU health certificate issued by an official vet
  • Appropriate CITES documentation (Annex A or B certificate, or breeding declaration)
  • Microchip or ring identification matching the documentation

Transport must comply with animal welfare regulations (adequate ventilation, temperature, food and water for the journey duration).

Outside the EU (Switzerland, UK, Norway, etc.)

For countries outside the EU, additional import permits are generally required. The buyer is typically responsible for obtaining the import permit from their country's wildlife authority. The seller provides an export permit where applicable. This is more complex but manageable with the right guidance — see our delivery page for more details.

Spain as a Source Country for Exotic Birds

Spain deserves special mention as one of Europe's most productive and well-regulated sources of captive-bred exotic parrots. The combination of climate (beneficial for breeding tropical species), established aviculture tradition and growing regulatory professionalism makes Spain an excellent country to source high-quality birds.

Questions to Ask Any Seller Before Buying

Regardless of where you are buying, ask these questions and judge the seller on how well they answer:

  1. Can you show me the CITES documentation for this specific bird?
  2. Is the bird captive-bred in your own aviaries?
  3. Can I see photos or video of the aviary and breeding conditions?
  4. When was the bird's last veterinary check, and can I see the certificate?
  5. Has the bird been hand-raised and socialized? For how long?
  6. Can I visit in person to see the bird before purchasing?
  7. What after-sale support do you provide?
  8. What food has the bird been eating and how should I transition it?

A reputable seller will welcome every one of these questions. A seller who deflects, becomes vague or pressures you to buy quickly is showing significant red flags.
