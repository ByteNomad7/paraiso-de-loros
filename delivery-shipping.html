🚚 Delivery & Shipping

We deliver exotic parrots safely across Spain and the EU. Learn about our transport methods, documentation, costs and what to expect when your bird arrives.

Our Approach to Safe Transport

Moving a live bird is a responsibility we take with absolute seriousness. Unlike shipping an inanimate product, transporting a parrot requires careful preparation of the bird, appropriate crating, route planning to minimize stress, and full compliance with animal welfare regulations.

We have developed our shipping protocols over years of experience, working with specialist avian transport services that understand the specific needs of exotic birds. Our birds arrive healthy, calm and ready to begin adjusting to their new home.

Delivery Options

✈️ Air Freight (EU & International)

For international buyers, we arrange air freight using IATA-approved live animal crates through airlines with established live animal programs. The bird is accompanied by a full documentation package including EU health certificate, CITES permits and airline-specific paperwork.

  • Most common method for EU buyers
  • Transit times: typically 4–24 hours
  • Temperature-controlled hold sections
  • Buyer may need to clear customs at destination

🚐 Ground Transport (Spain & Portugal)

For buyers within Spain and Portugal, we use specialist avian ground courier services with temperature-controlled vehicles and experienced handlers. This is often the lowest-stress option for the bird as it avoids airport procedures entirely.

  • Available throughout Spain and Portugal
  • Door-to-door delivery available
  • Typically 1–2 day transit
  • Best option for shorter distances

🚗 Collection in Person

Buyers who prefer it can arrange to collect their bird in person. This allows you to meet the bird in our environment, see where it was raised, and begin building rapport before the journey home. We encourage this for buyers within reasonable driving distance.

  • No transport stress for the bird
  • You see the aviaries and conditions firsthand
  • Direct handover of all documentation
  • Available by appointment

🤝 Accompanied Transport

For some high-value birds or long-distance international shipments, one of our team members can accompany the bird as cabin baggage or cargo supervision. This premium option ensures maximum oversight throughout transit.

  • Available for Hyacinth Macaw and similar
  • Maximum care and attention in transit
  • Additional cost applies
  • Arranged case by case

Step-by-Step Delivery Process

1
Confirm Your Order

After choosing your bird and paying the agreed deposit, we lock in the delivery timeline and begin preparing documentation.

2
Documentation Preparation

We prepare the EU health certificate (issued by our official vet), CITES export documentation (where applicable), and any destination-country import permits needed.

3
Pre-Travel Health Check

The bird receives a final veterinary check within 48 hours of transport to confirm fitness for travel and to validate the health certificate.

4
Crate Preparation

The bird is acclimated to its travel crate in advance. IATA-compliant crates are sized appropriately for the species and stocked with food and water for the journey.

5
Transit

The bird travels by the agreed method. We monitor the shipment and keep you updated throughout the journey.

6
Arrival & Handover

You receive the bird along with the complete documentation pack. We call to confirm safe arrival and give you settling-in guidance for the first 24–48 hours.

Documentation We Provide

Every shipment includes the following documentation as standard:

  • EU Veterinary Health Certificate — confirming the bird is fit for transport and free from notifiable diseases
  • CITES Export Permit / Annex A Certificate — legal documentation of the bird's captive-bred status and legal ownership
  • Ring or Microchip Certificate — identifying documentation for the individual bird
  • Breeding Records — provenance documentation showing captive breeding history
  • Care Guide — our practical guide for the first weeks with your new bird

For buyers outside the EU, we advise on additional import permits required by your country and assist with the application process where possible.

After Your Bird Arrives: The First 48 Hours

The first 48 hours after arrival are critical for a successful transition. Here is what we advise:

  • Place the bird in a quiet, warm room away from high traffic areas
  • Keep the cage covered partially for the first day to reduce stimulation
  • Offer familiar foods (we tell you what the bird has been eating)
  • Speak softly and calmly near the cage — do not try to force interaction
  • Monitor droppings, eating and drinking to confirm the bird is settling
  • Call us if you have any concerns — we are available
