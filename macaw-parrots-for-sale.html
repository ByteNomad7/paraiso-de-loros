Blue & Gold Macaw

The most popular large macaw in Spain and Europe. Striking blue and yellow plumage with a white face. Highly sociable, loves to talk and perform tricks.

Scarlet Macaw

One of the most visually stunning parrots on earth. Red, yellow and blue plumage make the Scarlet a real showstopper. Energetic and requires experienced handling.

Hyacinth Macaw

The largest flying parrot in the world. Cobalt blue plumage and gentle temperament earn the Hyacinth the title "gentle giant." Extremely rare and valuable.

  • Size: 100 cm / 1,200–1,700g
  • Lifespan: 50–60 years
  • Personality: gentle, intelligent, calm
  • Ideal for: dedicated enthusiasts

Green-Winged Macaw

Often called the "gentle macaw," the Green-Winged has a calmer, more tolerant personality than many other large macaws. Deep crimson body with green wing panels.

  • Size: 90 cm / 1,100–1,500g
  • Lifespan: 50–60 years
  • Personality: calm, affectionate, gentle
  • Ideal for: families, patient owners
🦜

Military Macaw

Predominantly olive-green with a red forehead and blue flight feathers. A more subdued talker but an excellent companion bird with a loyal temperament.

  • Ideal for: intermediate owners

What It Takes to Own a Macaw

Macaws are extraordinary birds but they come with significant responsibilities. Before buying a macaw parrot in Spain or anywhere in Europe, consider the following carefully:

Space Requirements

A large macaw needs a cage at minimum 150 × 120 × 180 cm — and that is just for sleeping and safe downtime. They need several hours of daily out-of-cage time to fly, explore and interact. If you live in a small apartment without outdoor space, a large macaw may not be the right choice. Smaller macaws (e.g., Hahn's) can be more apartment-friendly.

Noise

Macaws are loud. They vocalize naturally in the morning and evening and can call unexpectedly throughout the day. Neighbours and noise-sensitive environments need to be considered. This is one of the key differences in the macaw vs cockatoo comparison.

Commitment

Large macaws can live 60–80 years. Buying a Hyacinth Macaw or Blue & Gold at age 30 means potentially committing to care for them into your old age — and making provisions in your will for their long-term care.

Diet

Macaws need a varied diet rich in fresh vegetables, quality pellets, some fruit, nuts (especially for Hyacinths who eat palm nuts in the wild), and limited seeds. Access our full parrot care guide for detailed dietary advice.
